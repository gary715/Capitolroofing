All abbreviations must be mapped to a standard product or task before generating a material list.

| Abbreviation | Meaning |
|---|---|
| IB | IB Roof Systems PVC membrane (general) |
| 40M / 40 mil | 40 mil IB membrane |
| 50M / 50 mil | 50 mil IB membrane |
| 60M / 60 mil | 60 mil IB membrane |
| 80M / 80 mil | 80 mil IB membrane (premium grade) |

**Notes:**
- A "60 mil - 1 Roll" listed alongside 80 mil field membrane typically means a **Detail/Flashing Membrane roll** (40' rolls used for flashings and repairs), not additional field membrane.
- Field membrane is listed in **squares (SQ)**; detail rolls are listed as **rolls (40' each)**.
- If field notes list 60 mil with a roll count (not squares), treat as Detail/Flashing Membrane.

## IB PVC Clad Metal (Sheet Metal Types)

All metal listed below is **IB PVC clad metal** unless a gauge (e.g. .032) is specified for non-PVC-clad aluminum.
PVC clad metal heat-welds directly to the IB field membrane.

**Cover Strip Rule:** IB Cover Strip is required when using perimeter metal (GS and DE only). See Derived Materials Rules.

| Abbreviation | Spreadsheet Name | Meaning | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| EDG / DE | DE 4"x4" IB | Drip Edge (edge metal) — IB PVC clad | Perimeter edge termination |
| GS / GSx | GS4, GS5, GS6, etc. | Gravel Stop — number = face height in inches | Raised edge metal (e.g. GS4 = 4", GS5 = 5", GS6 = 6") |
| COP / Cap | Coping Cap | Coping cap — covers top of parapet wall | Profile includes face + top + back leg |
| REG | Reglet | Counterflashing at walls | — |
| STEP | Step Flashing | Step flashing at vertical wall intersections | — |
| VLY | Valley Metal | At roof valleys / transitions | — |
| BASE | Base Flashing | At walls and curbs | — |
| Term bar / Term Metal | Term Metal | Termination bar — mechanically fastened at edges and walls | Same as TMB above |
| Sep 3x3 | Sep 3x3 | Separator metal 3"x3" | Does NOT trigger cover strip rule |
| Sep 4x4 | Sep 4x4 | Separator metal 4"x4" | Does NOT trigger cover strip rule |
| Service Metal | Service Metal | Service/maintenance metal | Does NOT trigger cover strip rule |
| Pitch Pocket | Pitch Pocket | Pitch pocket — metal box | Heights noted (e.g. "5" High", "10 LF" = linear footage) |

### Metal Gauge Notation
- **.032** = .032 gauge aluminum (thicker, structural — coping caps, large drip edges)
- **.024** = .024 gauge aluminum (standard weight)
- When gauge is listed (e.g. ".032 DE 4x4 kick"), the number before the type is the aluminum thickness

### Metal Profile Notation
- **4x4** = 4 inch face x 4 inch flange
- **5 1/2"** = 5.5 inch width (e.g. coping cap face)
- **"plus kick"** = a bent lip at the bottom edge of drip edge to direct water away from wall
- **"High"** = vertical height of metal (e.g. pitch pocket "5" High")

## Rules for Using This Legend

1. Every abbreviation on a field sheet **must** resolve to an item in this legend
2. If an abbreviation is not found, flag it for review — do NOT assume
3. If a symbol appears without a number, return to the field sheet and look for a nearby note
4. Pipe counts must be exact — confirm total before generating material list
5. Any item marked with ★ must appear in the estimate notes section
6. Metal listed without LF must be flagged — **LF is required to calculate cover strip**
7. "Re-Roof" alone does not confirm job type — check work order or notes for overlay vs tear-off
8. Multiple roof sections in a sketch are additive — sum all sections for total squares
9. If total squares in sketch does not match total squares in work order, flag the discrepancy
10. **Cover strip applies ONLY to perimeter IB PVC clad metal (GS and DE).** Separator metal and service metal do NOT trigger the cover strip rule.
11. Pipe boots are called "cones" on the spreadsheet (A-Cone, B-Cone, C-Cone, D-Cone, E-Cone). Field notes may use just the letter.
12. "Term bar" and "Term Metal" are the same product — use "Term Metal" as the standard name.
13. Skylight model numbers follow the FCM format — FCM XXYY where XX = width and YY = height in inches (e.g. FCM 2222 = 22"x22").
