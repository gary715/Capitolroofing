# Corners and Detail Components

## Outside Corners (OS)

- **Prefabricated** by IB Roof Systems
- Used at **outside angles** on the roof — where two vertical surfaces meet at an exterior corner
- Common locations: chimney corners, skylight corners, parapet wall corners, curb corners
- **4 corners per unit** (each rectangular penetration/curb has 4 outside corners)
- High counts are normal on buildings with multiple package units + skylights
  - Example: 8 AC package units + 2 skylights = (8 + 2) x 4 = 40 OS corners

## Inside Corners (IS)

- **Prefabricated** by IB Roof Systems
- Used at **inside angles** — where the roof meets a wall in a corner
- The intersection of the horizontal (roof) and vertical (wall) surfaces on the inside
- Common locations: parapet wall inside corners, curb inside corners, equipment base inside corners

## Corner Count Formula

For any rectangular roof penetration or curb:
- **4 outside corners** (OS) if it sits in the field
- **Inside corners** (IS) at wall-to-roof transitions in concave angles

For a building perimeter with parapet walls:
- **Inside corners** at each change of direction (where wall meets wall at the roof line)

## Seam Plates

- **2" barbed seam plates** installed under the 6" membrane overlap
- Used on every seam before heat welding

## T-Patches (80 mil only)

- Required at every point where membrane seams intersect
- Prevents the intersecting weld from pulling open
- Not required for 50 mil or 60 mil installations
